Common questions

What expecting parents ask most

I've heard the chance of using cord blood is low. Is it worth it?
That's a fair concern — but the landscape of medicine is shifting fast. You aren't just banking for today's treatments; you're investing in what's coming next. Medical science uncovers new applications every year.
If my family has no history of illness, is this necessary?
Banking isn't just for hereditary conditions — it's increasingly about regenerative medicine and treating injuries that can happen to anyone. These cells serve as a biological backup for your child.
If I miss this window, can I still get access to donor stem cells?
Yes, but it comes with a significant cost. A single cord blood unit for transplant can cost $35,000 to $50,000. Banking at birth secures a perfect genetic match and avoids that burden.
