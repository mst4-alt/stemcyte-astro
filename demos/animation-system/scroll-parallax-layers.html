Why cord blood matters

Cord blood is collected from the umbilical cord immediately after birth. It contains powerful stem cells that can be used in transplants to treat over 80 diseases, including leukemia, lymphoma, and inherited metabolic disorders.

Unlike bone marrow, cord blood does not require a perfect match between donor and recipient, making it a versatile and valuable medical resource for your family.

Protection for generations

Stem cells stored today can serve your child, their siblings, and potentially their children. It's a form of biological insurance that grows more valuable as medicine advances.

The science keeps advancing

Every year, researchers discover new applications for cord blood stem cells. From regenerative medicine to immunotherapy, the possibilities continue to expand far beyond what was imaginable even a decade ago.

By banking today, you ensure access to treatments that may not yet exist -- but almost certainly will.
