The Journey of a Stem Cell

From a single cell to a living system — how cord blood stem cells carry the potential to transform medicine.

Scroll to explore

Every life begins with a single extraordinary cell

Stem cells are the foundation of all human biology. They possess the unique ability to become any type of cell in the body — and when preserved at birth, they retain that potential for decades. Follow the journey from one cell to a complete biological network.

01 — A single cell

Where it all begins

Every person on Earth started as a single stem cell. These remarkable cells contain the blueprint for everything — every organ, every tissue, every system in your body.

02 — The first division

Multiplication begins

Within hours of collection, stem cells begin their work. They divide and multiply, each new cell carrying the same extraordinary potential as the original.

03 — Building connections

A network takes shape

Stem cells don't work alone. They communicate, forming networks that coordinate growth and repair. This intercellular signaling is what makes transplants effective.

04 — Specialization begins

Differentiation

Some stem cells differentiate — becoming red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets. Others remain undifferentiated, ready to become whatever the body needs.

05 — A living system

The full network

What began as a single cell becomes a complete, functioning biological system. This is the power your baby is born with — and the power cord blood banking preserves.

Preserve their potential

Cord blood banking captures this extraordinary biology at its most powerful moment — the day your baby is born. These stem cells can be stored for decades, ready when your family needs them most.
