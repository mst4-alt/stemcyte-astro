How Cord Blood Banking Works

A step-by-step look at how we collect, process, and preserve your baby's cord blood stem cells.

Scroll to explore ↓

Collection is simple

After your baby is born and the cord is clamped, our medical team collects the remaining blood from the umbilical cord. The process is painless and takes just a few minutes, with no risk to mother or child.

Your stem cells are processed

Within 48 hours of collection, your cord blood arrives at our Baldwin Park laboratory. Our team processes and tests each unit to ensure viability, then prepares it for long-term cryogenic storage.

Stored for a lifetime

Your baby's cord blood is cryopreserved in our state-of-the-art facility, ready if your family ever needs it. Research shows properly stored cord blood remains viable indefinitely, giving you peace of mind for decades to come.
