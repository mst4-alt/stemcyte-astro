THE SIMPLE VERSION

Cord blood is the blood left in your baby's umbilical cord after birth.

It's collected in under five minutes, right after delivery, completely painless for both you and your baby. What makes it extraordinary is what's inside: stem cells that can rebuild a human blood and immune system from scratch. These cells are already used in transplant medicine worldwide. And they can only be collected once, at the moment of birth.

WHAT'S INSIDE

The cells that build your blood and immune system

Cord blood contains hematopoietic stem cells. In plain English: these are the master cells that produce every red blood cell, white blood cell, and platelet in your body. Think of them as the factory that makes your blood.

🧬

They migrate to bone marrow

When transplanted, cord blood stem cells travel through the bloodstream and find their way to the bone marrow, where blood cells are made.

🔄

They engraft and rebuild

Once there, they engraft and begin producing an entirely new blood and immune system. This process is called hematopoietic reconstitution.

✨

Younger, purer, more tolerant

Collected at birth, these cells have zero exposure to aging, illness, or environmental damage. They also cause less immune rejection than adult donor cells.

WHAT IT CAN DO

Honest answers, not inflated claims

You may have heard "cord blood treats 80+ diseases." That's true for transplants using donor cord blood. Here's what the evidence actually shows, broken into three tiers.

Established

Standard transplant medicine

Allogeneic transplants for blood cancers, bone marrow failure, immune deficiencies, metabolic disorders, and sickle cell disease.

Leukemia Lymphoma Sickle Cell Disease Thalassemia Krabbe Disease Fanconi Anemia SCID + many more
Clinical Trials

Active investigation

Cerebral palsy, stroke, autism (ongoing, inconclusive), HIV remission.

Cerebral Palsy Stroke Autism HIV Remission

StemCyte cord blood was part of the research behind the first woman to achieve HIV remission through a cord blood transplant.

Early Research

Preclinical & emerging

Early-stage studies exploring potential applications across neurodegenerative and injury contexts.
